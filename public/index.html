Scope

Problem Statement & Scope of Work

Why this PoC matters and what we will deliver in 4 weeks.

Problem Statement

Amtech Software's support team operates under significant manual overhead with no intelligent deflection layer in place. Every customer case, approximately 1,500 per month, hits the support team directly. Roughly 35–40% of those tickets are resolvable through existing knowledge that customers simply aren't finding on their own. Manual triage and routing create additional operational burden, and the bug-to-Jira escalation workflow requires human intervention at every step, with no automated handoff between support and engineering.

In Scope

Three Use Cases

This PoC will focus on demonstrating core value through three use cases.

Use Case 1

Computer for Customers

DevRev's AI-powered widget (PLuG) will be embedded in a simulated version of the Amtech Engage support portal, accessible to authenticated customers. The agent will be grounded in the 2,000 Salesforce KB articles ingested during setup, allowing it to answer questions and resolve common issues before a case is submitted. When escalation is required, the agent will first gather the necessary information before creating the case, ensuring a smooth handoff to the support team.

Use Case 2

Triage — Intelligent Case Intake

Using pre-defined routing logic defined jointly with the Amtech team, a workflow automation will route each incoming ticket to the appropriate support team based on its content. Classified tickets will be available for review within DevRev.

Use Case 3

Computer as Copilot for the Support Team

Support reps will have access to Computer as an internal assistant, grounded in the full ingested dataset — 10,000 cases, 43,000 case comments, 2,000 KB articles, and 1,000 Jira issues — across three sub-use cases:

  • Similar case & knowledge search — Agents can query past cases, comments, and documentation in natural language to find relevant resolutions, without switching between tools.
  • Customer context & meeting prep — Before or during any customer interaction, agents can request a full account briefing — open cases, active projects, and recent escalations.
  • Trend detection — Managers and support leads can query patterns across the ingested ticket history to identify customers submitting unusual volumes of how-to questions, modules generating recurring issues, or case spikes following a recent go-live.

Success Criteria

How We'll Measure Success

To determine if the scope of work is successful, Amtech Software will validate that DevRev was able to deliver on these three outcomes.

01

Deflect customer support inquiries through self-service

Computer accurately answers customer questions using Amtech's existing knowledge base.

Target: ≥30% inquiries resolved without case creation
02

Route cases to the correct support team

When Computer cannot resolve an inquiry, the case is automatically classified and routed to the right team with full context preserved.

Target: ≥60% correctly classified and routed
03

Deliver accurate and trustworthy responses via Computer copilot

Support agents can retrieve relevant information from past cases, KB articles, and Jira issues through natural language queries. Feedback collected via thumbs up / thumbs down on each response.

  • Thumbs up — response was correct, trustworthy, and useful.
  • Thumbs down — agent selects a reason: answer was wrong, not useful, Computer should have said it didn't know, or other.
Target: ≥40% thumbs up
